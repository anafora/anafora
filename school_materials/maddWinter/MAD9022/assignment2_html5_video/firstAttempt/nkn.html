Creating a WebPage with a Video Background

Take a video clip of 20 - 40 seconds in length and convert it into the following formats - mp4, ogg, and webm. There are many online video converters or ones that you can download for free. One good example is http://www.oxelon.com/media_converter.html Another for those of you on Mac is FFmpeg https://www.ffmpeg.org/. There are other converters listed on this page - Converting Between Formats

You must own the video so, use your phone to take the video.

Build an HTML5 web page to play your video using the HTML video element. - HTML Video

Use any tool you like to extract a screen grab from the video to use as the poster attribute for the video. Remember to get the aspect ratio the same for your image and your video, otherwise it will look strange when being resized.

Try to optimize your video to make it a smaller file size. When you do the conversion keep the dimensions and resolution down. HD or even SD is sufficient for a background. We will be resizing the video to stretch it to fill our layout. We will be using an overlay to hide the loss in quality.

Your video should NOT have too much motion. Make a recording of a large crowd, rain or snow falling, a sleeping pet, wind blowing some garbage, highway traffic from an overpass, etc. The key is to create a video that will not distract users from the content on your page. 

Layout

The video player should be responsive and resize to fill 100% of the screen width (or your layout width). 

You can build your layout in one of two ways:

1. Use media queries to create three break points. At each break point create a fixed-width layout. At each layout size you need to be sure that the video fills with width of your layout but does NOT exceed the width. It should also be at least one third of the height of your page. Use hard coded values for the width and height of the video container with this approach.

2. You can use percentages for the width or height of your video. Have the video fill the entire width of the screen and use auto for the height of your video. 

 

With either solution, you need to have an overlay area with some your page heading and sub heading.

Make sure that the heading section includes a background that will work as an overlay for the video. Background overlays can be found at the site provided on this page - HTML Video Backgrounds

The content in the area below the headings should have a solid colour background and include two columns of Lorem Ipsum style text.

 

Your layout will be tested in Chrome at a wide variety of screen sizes. Make sure all your content is responsive. Font-sizes should be appropriate for screen dimensions. Content should be aligned and padded.
